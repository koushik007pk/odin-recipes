Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that consists of layers of wide, flat pasta sheets alternated with various fillings and sauces. Traditionally, the filling includes a combination of ground meats, such as beef or pork, mixed with a rich tomato sauce (like ragù or bolognese), and layered with béchamel (a creamy white sauce) or ricotta cheese. Mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses are often added for extra flavor and a golden, bubbly top.

Each layer is built up by stacking the pasta sheets, spreading a generous amount of sauce, and adding cheese or other ingredients like vegetables (such as spinach or mushrooms), depending on the recipe. After layering, the lasagna is baked in the oven until the top becomes golden and the cheese is melted and slightly crispy.

Lasagna is loved for its hearty, comforting flavors, and its ability to feed a crowd. Variations exist, such as vegetarian versions, seafood lasagna, or lighter options made with different vegetables or gluten-free pasta. Its rich, filling nature makes it a popular choice for both family meals and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz uncooked lasagna noodles (such as penne or rigatoni)
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Romano cheese (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Gorgonzola cheese (optional)
  • 1/4 cup grated balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup grated fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup grated fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup grated fresh oregano

Steps

  • Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  • Add the ground meats, Parmesan, mozzarella, and Romano cheeses, and cook for 2-3 minutes, until melted and bubbly.
  • Add the balsamic vinegar, parsley, basil, and oregano, and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until fragrant.
  • Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  • Once the lasagna is cooked, transfer it to a wire rack and set aside.
